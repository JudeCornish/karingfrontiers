Background:

Implementation of the Institute of Higher Education under the Karen Education Department (KED) was initiated in Mar NerPlaw, the Karen National Union Headquarters in 1982. The initial aim of the Institute was to provide high school graduates with an opportunity to further their education in specific fields of studies.

The State Peace and Development Council’s (SPDC) and Democratic Karen Buddhist Army’s (DKBA) military activities in Karen State reached its peak in 1995, and as the results, thousands of civilian population moved to the Thai side of the Thailand-Burma border to take temporary refuge in different refugee camps, allowing them to set up their own education system. There are a total of seven Karen refugee camps located in Ratchburi province, Kanchanaburi province, Tak province, and Mae Hongson province respectively.

As the number of high school graduates increased, community leaders and educational personnel realized that there was a need to set up post-ten schools or tertiary education programs in order to provide an opportunity for high school graduates to continue their studies in the camps. However, each post-ten school had its own aim and objectives according to the need of the community, and there were very limited cooperation and link between these programs.

Therefore, with the intention of recognizing the efforts of these post-ten programs have made, the Institute of Higher Education, currently under the Karen Refugee Committee-Education Entity is initiating an action plan by promoting the existing post-ten programs and encouraging each to take initiative steps, extending their programming according to the needs and requirements of their communities.

The Mae Ra Moe Junior College (MRMJC) the Mae Ra Moe Junior College was situated in Mae Ra Moe Karen Refugee Camp in Mae Hongson Province (Thailand).It was formerly known as the Agriculture Studies Programme (ASP) which was established in 1998 in Mae Ra Moe refugee camp. At that time, the program’s emphasis was only on the teaching of agriculture and animal husbandry. In the academic year of 2003-2004, when school name was changed to Further Education Course (FEC), another stream of studies called Special Language Course (SLC) under the FEC was added. In 2008, when the school came under the IHE education system, the name was changed to Mae Ra Moe Junior College (MRMJC) and has utilized IHE curriculum and policies since then.

Current objectives:

  1. To prepare leadership for all branches, organisations and departments in our communities.
  2. To adequately quality graduates for a position in recommended field area(s) in Karen Communities.
  3. To provide broad options in lifework through quality preparation for career in profession, public services and business.
  4. To enable students to acquire high levels of knowledge.
  5. To promote critical analysis and testing of ideas and creativity.
  6. To assist students in developing their capacity to think and to interact with the problems of life, clearness of thought and commitment to their convictions.
  7. To support the authority as a means of maintaing order and protection of persons and property.
  8. To uphold principles of healthful living not only in courses taught but also in regulation of the school program and management.
